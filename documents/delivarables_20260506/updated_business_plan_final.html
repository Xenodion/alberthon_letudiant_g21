I. Strategic Vision

The strategic vision guiding this three-year plan is the repositioning of L'Étudiant as the primary national infrastructure for student-intention intelligence. Instead of functioning as an organisation that sells leads generated through fairs and digital forms, L'Étudiant aims to become a provider of continuous, compliant and predictive insight products. This shift is driven by structural changes in the digital environment, including the decline of third-party identifiers and the growing importance of first-party data for recruitment, outreach and strategic planning. By placing behavioural and recency-based intelligence at the centre of its value proposition, L'Étudiant defines a future in which institutions depend on it not merely for visibility or lead volume but for high-resolution analytical guidance.

II. Baseline and Initial Conditions

L'Étudiant enters this transformation with considerable strengths. Its platforms attract more than 7.3 million monthly visitors and over 34 million page views, and its 76 annual fairs draw more than two million attendees. These interactions provide a continuous stream of behavioural and declarative data across multiple touchpoints. The organisation also benefits from a highly trusted brand and long-standing relationships with educational institutions.

However, the existing data infrastructure is fragmented. Manual cleaning, inconsistent deduplication, irregular ingestion cycles and ad-hoc delivery formats limit the commercial exploitation of these assets. GDPR compliance, although fundamental, remains a labour-intensive constraint. Recruitment markets have become more competitive and more data-intensive, meaning that institutions increasingly require timely updates, predictive scoring and granular segmentations. This baseline reveals a clear gap between L'Étudiant's data potential and its current data product maturity.

III. Three-Year Roadmap

The three-year roadmap advances through clearly delineated phases: stabilisation, expansion and consolidation. Each phase builds upon the previous one to ensure that operational readiness, market adoption and analytical sophistication evolve coherently.

Year 1 — Foundations: Infrastructure, Governance and Standardisation

The first year focuses on stabilising the technical and organisational infrastructure required to support recurring data products. The central components of the ingestion pipeline — normalisation, deduplication, freshness calculation, intention scoring and compliance filtering — are fully automated to ensure repeatability. The scoring algorithm is formalised: freshness, intention and completeness are operationalised into a composite grade (A/B/C), and GDPR filters are codified into mechanical validation steps. A robust data governance framework is introduced. Operationally, Year 1 aims to convert a portion of existing institutional clients into the Standard Access subscription tier at €800/month. The goal is not yet aggressive revenue expansion but the establishment of a reliable first cohort of 8 Standard and 3 Premium subscribers who validate the viability of the product.

Outcome of Year 1A functional data infrastructure, a trained internal organisation, 11 paying subscribers across Standard and Premium tiers, and verifiable proof of product reliability and compliance. Revenue: €179,300. EBITDA: €67,784 (38% margin).

Year 2 — Expansion: Scaling Adoption and Strengthening Market Position

In the second year, with the infrastructure stable, L'Étudiant focuses on expanding adoption across its addressable market. The Premium Access tier at €2,500/month becomes the engine of commercial growth. Institutions begin to integrate weekly-refreshed intention grades into their CRM systems. The API capabilities offered in the Premium tier enable real-time synchronisation with institutional operational tools, transforming L'Étudiant's data from a static asset into a dynamic strategic input. The Barometer Dashboard expands into a national reference for monitoring programme interest, regional trends, academic fields and year-to-year behavioural shifts.

Outcome of Year 221 subscribers (10 Standard, 9 Premium, 2 Sur Mesure). Premium becomes dominant revenue driver. Revenue: €506,000. EBITDA: €318,280 (63% margin).

Year 3 — Consolidation: Customisation and Market Leadership

The third year consolidates L'Étudiant's identity as a fully operational Data Product Company. The Sur Mesure tier at €5,000+/month becomes central to the commercial strategy. Institutions with intense competition for students — business schools, engineering schools, health-related programmes — seek bespoke models incorporating programme-specific signals and longitudinal behavioural analytics. Recurring subscription revenue becomes a structurally important component of L'Étudiant's financial model.

Outcome of Year 331 subscribers (9 Standard, 16 Premium, 6 Sur Mesure). Premium + Sur Mesure represent 71% of subscriber base. Revenue: €953,900. EBITDA: €674,432 (71% margin).

IV. Operational Plan

The operational plan extends across the three years and ensures that L'Étudiant becomes capable of delivering continuous, compliant and predictive data products at scale. In the first year, the organisation focuses on standardising its data architecture, formalising quality-assurance procedures and embedding GDPR compliance into the pipeline. The second year expands analytical capabilities, enhances segmentation depth and strengthens institutional support. The third year refines the infrastructure and enables custom analytical frameworks, ensuring that institutions receive not only data but contextualised insight. Each year reinforces the organisation's technological and procedural maturity, anchoring the transformation in operational stability.

V. Commercial and Revenue Strategy

The revenue model is structured around three subscription tiers corresponding to progressively deeper levels of analytical value, complemented by a transactional No-Show Intelligence Report product.

Over three years, the subscriber mix shifts from a majority of Standard-tier clients to a balanced distribution across all tiers, with Premium and Sur Mesure gaining prominence as institutions mature in their use of first-party behavioural data. A single Standard-to-Premium upgrade generates €20,400 in additional annual recurring revenue per client.

VI. Market Development Strategy

The market development strategy leverages L'Étudiant's recognised position within the educational ecosystem. Its long-standing trust capital and its extensive behavioural dataset — over 76 annual fairs, 7.3 million monthly visitors — provide a competitive advantage that cannot be replicated by data brokers without comparable first-party coverage. As institutions increasingly seek behavioural and intention-based indicators, L'Étudiant's predictive scoring and segmentation become essential for programme-level decision-making. The three-year horizon allows the organisation to progressively educate institutions about the strategic value of behavioural analytics, supporting the transition from occasional lead purchases to subscription-based engagement.

VII. Risk Assessment and Mitigation

The transformation introduces regulatory, operational and adoption-related risks. GDPR-related risks are mitigated by embedding compliance into the verification pipeline from the outset — three automatic filters (commercial opt-in, age verification, active-status check) run before any profile reaches an activation output. Operational risks linked to scale and frequency are mitigated through automation and versioning of the ingestion pipeline. Adoption risks are reduced through targeted client support and the demonstrable value of refreshed intention-based datasets, evidenced by the working Barometer Dashboard. The three-year sequencing ensures that risks are addressed progressively and proportionally to the organisation's technical and commercial maturity.

VIII. Expected Outcomes

At the end of the three-year period, L'Étudiant will have completed its transition into a Data Product Company built on recurring revenue, automated intelligence processing and deep institutional relationships. The organisation will operate a unified and predictive data pipeline, support institutions through both standardised and custom analytical offerings, and maintain a stable income generated by Standard, Premium and Sur Mesure subscriptions. L'Étudiant will have moved beyond transactional lead delivery and established itself as a national leader in behavioural and intention-based educational intelligence.

Variable 2 — Premium Access Tier Price

Price elasticity is unknown. The market currently pays €0.30–0.60 per raw lead (list rental). The base case of €2,500/month implies a client paying the equivalent of €3.12 per scored lead for 200 leads per week — roughly 5× the current list-rental price. The perceived value premium of weekly-refreshed behavioural intelligence over a static list must be validated through client pilots in Year 1.

XIII. Build vs. Partner Framework

L'Étudiant's question: should the lead scoring model be built in-house or rely on a third-party enrichment partner (Cognism, Clearbit, Lusha)?

Option A — Build In-house
+Exclusive behavioural signals (chatbot, fairs, site) — non-reproducible by any third party. Core differentiator.
+Full model control — adapts to seasonal cycles (back-to-school, Parcoursup, fair calendar)
+Native GDPR compliance — no additional DPA required, data stays within L'Étudiant's BigQuery perimeter
+85–93% gross margin preserved — no third-party licence cost
+Strategic asset — model becomes a barrier to entry over time
−Requires in-house data science competency or a hire
−Longer time-to-market for advanced ML models (3–6 months)
★ Recommended
Option B — Hybrid (Build core + Partner enrichment)
+Core scoring model in-house on proprietary signals — differentiator preserved
+Geographic enrichment via INSEE open data (free, no DPA complexity) for the ~3% of profiles without a region
+Commercial partner (Cognism, Clearbit) only if Life-moment API scales and B2B clients explicitly request cross-domain signals in Y2+
+Loose coupling — partner can be replaced without rebuilding the core model
−Licence cost if commercial partner activated: €500–3,000/month depending on volume
−DPA required with any third party — GDPR due diligence adds 4–8 weeks

Final Recommendation

Two-stage hybrid architecture: (1) Launch with the current in-house pipeline on BigQuery — sufficient for MVP and the commercial pilot. (2) From production onwards, evaluate INSEE open-data enrichment for profiles without a region. Commercial partners (Cognism, Clearbit) are relevant only in Y2 if Life-moment API clients explicitly request cross-domain signals — a decision to revisit at that point, not now.
